Shigeru Miyamoto

The creator of some of the most iconic game franchises of all time

Some important date in the life of Shigeru Miyamoto:

  • 1952 - Born in the Japanese town of Sonobe in the Kyoyo Prefecture
  • 1977 - Hired as an apprentice in the planning department of Nintendo
  • 1981 - Directed and designed Donkey Kong and its sequels the following years
  • 1983 - Directed and designed Mario Bros, one of the most influential video game of all time
  • 1985 - Directed, designed and produced Super Mario Bros
  • 1986 - Directed, designed and produced The Legend of Zelda, an innovative game with nonlinear gameplay and expansive world which will be the first episode of one of the most inonic video game's saga
  • 1989 - Became head of the Software Development Department of Nintendo EAD (Nintendo Entertainment Analysis & Development)
  • 1991 - Produced The Legend of Zelda: A Link to the Past, a very important sequel introducing key elements of the franchise like the Master sword, the alternate or parallel world, etc
  • 1996 - Directed and produced Super Mario 64, regularly quote as one of the most revolutionary video games of all time
  • 1998 - Designed, produced and supervised The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time, the highest-rated video game in history with a Metacritic score of 99 out of 100
  • 2001 - Produced Luigi's Mansion out of a demo based on one of his very own ideas
  • 2001 - The same year he also produced Pikmin, a new franchise based on his experiences gardening.
  • 2006 - Release of the Wii, a home video game console focused on motion control gaming. Miyamoto played a major role in its developement
  • 2006 - Produced and partly directed The Legend of Zelda: Twilight Princess. Prior to the release of Breath of the Wild in 2018, it was the best-selling Zelda Game
  • 2015 - Produced Splatoon alongside Katsuya Eguchi
